# Item 2: Vercel – Open SDK strategy

Source: [https://vercel.com/blog/open-sdk-strategy](https://vercel.com/blog/open-sdk-strategy)

Summary:
Vercel outlines its new "Open SDK" strategy, reaffirming its commitment to open source, portability, and freedom of choice for developers. The post details Vercel’s approach to building frameworks and SDKs that are open by default, loosely coupled, and platform-agnostic—including Next.js, Nuxt, Svelte, and others. It emphasizes that innovation will happen first on Vercel, but tools will be generalized for broader use. The strategy is a response to concerns about vendor lock-in and aims to foster a healthier, more open web ecosystem.

Key takeaways:
- Vercel commits to permissive open source licensing and building tools that work beyond its own platform.
- Next.js, Nuxt, Svelte, and other frameworks will remain portable and not locked into Vercel infrastructure.
- The strategy is shaped by real-world developer feedback and recent product decisions.
- The approach is intended to build trust and long-term loyalty through openness, not lock-in.
