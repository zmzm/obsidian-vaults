# Item 7: Why TSRX isn't just your Favorite Templating Language

Source: [https://hackmd.io/@0u1u3zEAQAO0iYWVAStEvw/Hk2Jiv8T-l](https://hackmd.io/@0u1u3zEAQAO0iYWVAStEvw/Hk2Jiv8T-l)

Summary:
TSRX is introduced as a potential JSX successor, offering a more statically analyzable, flexible, and stateful templating approach for React, SolidJS, and Ripple. The article discusses the mechanical advantages of TSRX, such as composability, use of JavaScript statements for control flow, and the ability to place hooks in conditionals. It contrasts TSRX’s design with JSX and other template DSLs, highlighting benefits for optimization, ergonomics, and component state management.

Key takeaways:
- TSRX enables static analysis and flexible control flow using plain JavaScript, not just expressions.
- Supports hooks in conditionals, improving ergonomics and reducing boilerplate.
- Designed for composability and stateful templates, blending ideas from JSX and SFCs.
- Early alpha available for React and other frameworks; not a drop-in replacement yet.
