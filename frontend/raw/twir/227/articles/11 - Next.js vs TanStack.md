# Item 12: Next.js vs TanStack

Source: [https://www.kylegill.com/essays/next-vs-tanstack/](https://www.kylegill.com/essays/next-vs-tanstack/)

Summary:
This opinion piece compares Next.js and TanStack, highlighting Next.js’s strengths in out-of-the-box features and high-scale optimizations, but criticizing its complexity, proprietary APIs, and steep learning curve. The author prefers TanStack (with Vite) for its simplicity, flexibility, and transparent abstractions, arguing that most projects don’t need Next.js’s advanced features.

Key takeaways:
- Next.js excels at high-scale, optimized apps but introduces complexity and vendor lock-in.
- Many Next.js APIs are overkill for typical projects and tied to Vercel’s services.
- TanStack + Vite offers simpler, more transparent abstractions and better dev experience.
- Debugging and learning Next.js can be challenging due to its layers and conventions.
