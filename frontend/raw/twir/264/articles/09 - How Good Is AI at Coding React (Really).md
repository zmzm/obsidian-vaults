# Item 9: How Good Is AI at Coding React (Really)?

Source: [https://addyo.substack.com/p/how-good-is-ai-at-coding-react-really](https://addyo.substack.com/p/how-good-is-ai-at-coding-react-really)

Summary:
This data-driven article analyzes AI coding benchmarks for React, finding that AI excels at isolated tasks (like scaffolding components) but struggles with multi-step integration and complex state management. The author emphasizes that developer context, prompt specificity, and structured workflows are key to getting value from AI, especially as complexity increases.

Key takeaways:
- AI achieves ~40% success on isolated React tasks, dropping to ~25% for complex, multi-step work.
- Effectiveness is highest on mainstream stacks (React, TypeScript, Tailwind, shadcn/ui).
- Human oversight, explicit requirements, and context engineering are critical for quality results.
- AI is best used for first drafts and scaffolding, not for complex integrations without supervision.
