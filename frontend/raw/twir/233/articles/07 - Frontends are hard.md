# Item 8: Frontends are hard

Source: [https://sst.dev/blog/frontends-are-hard](https://sst.dev/blog/frontends-are-hard)

Summary:
This post analyzes why modern frontend frameworks (Next.js, Svelte, Astro, etc.) are difficult to deploy, citing complex infrastructure, rapid framework evolution, and limited support from major cloud providers. It describes how open-source projects like SST and OpenNext are addressing these challenges by decoupling build adapters from infrastructure and collaborating with framework authors. The article advocates for open source and community-driven solutions to keep up with the evolving frontend landscape.

Key takeaways:
- Modern frontends require multi-faceted infrastructure (SSR, APIs, edge, CDN), making deployment complex.
- Major cloud providers lag in supporting the latest frameworks and features.
- Open-source adapters and infrastructure (e.g., OpenNext) enable broader, up-to-date support.
- Community collaboration is key to keeping deployment tooling current and flexible.
