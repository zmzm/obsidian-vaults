Summary:
This podcast episode features members of the Next.js team discussing the new Adapters API, which enables Next.js apps to run across multiple platforms (e.g., Cloudflare, Netlify) and reduces vendor lock-in. The conversation covers caching strategies, performance optimizations, the role of Cache Components, and the evolution of bundling from Webpack to TurboPack. The guests also discuss the challenges of supporting multiple runtimes, the future of bundler standardization, and lessons learned from building TurboPack.

Key takeaways:
- The Adapters API allows Next.js to run on diverse platforms, addressing vendor lock-in concerns.
- Caching strategies and Cache Components are central to performance optimization in Next.js.
- TurboPack is the next-generation bundler, offering improved performance and incremental compilation.
- The episode provides insights into infrastructure, runtime challenges, and the evolution of the Next.js ecosystem.
