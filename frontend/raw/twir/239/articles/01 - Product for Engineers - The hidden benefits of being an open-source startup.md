# Item 1: Product for Engineers - The hidden benefits of being an open-source startup

Source: [https://go.posthog.com/twir-jun18](https://go.posthog.com/twir-jun18)

Summary:
This article explores how PostHog’s open-source model has shaped its product, culture, and business strategy. It details the advantages of being open source—such as differentiation, talent acquisition, and trust-building—with practical examples from PostHog’s journey. The piece also acknowledges challenges, like self-hosting complexity and differing definitions of “open source.” The overall message is that open source can be a powerful lever for growth and transparency in developer-focused products.

Key takeaways:
- Open source status helps products stand out in crowded markets, driving early adoption and community support.
- It improves hiring by allowing candidates to preview and contribute to the codebase, increasing alignment and transparency.
- Public development processes and codebases foster trust with developer users and buyers.
- There are trade-offs, such as self-hosting complexity and debates over what “open source” means.
