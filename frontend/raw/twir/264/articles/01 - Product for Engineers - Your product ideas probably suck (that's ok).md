# Item 1: Product for Engineers - Your product ideas probably suck (that's ok)

Source: [https://go.posthog.com/twir-jan14](https://go.posthog.com/twir-jan14)

Summary:
This article shares a practical playbook for validating product ideas, emphasizing the importance of focusing on real problems rather than jumping straight into building solutions. It details how PostHog’s founders iterated through multiple ideas, using user interviews and problem validation to avoid wasted effort. The guide covers effective outreach, asking the right questions, and common pitfalls in the validation process.

Key takeaways:
- Start with a list of real problems, ideally ones you or your target users have experienced.
- Validation requires direct user interviews; don’t rely on superficial feedback.
- Avoid common mistakes: asking leading questions, targeting the wrong audience, or overcomplicating validation.
- Pivot quickly if you don’t see genuine user pain or excitement.
