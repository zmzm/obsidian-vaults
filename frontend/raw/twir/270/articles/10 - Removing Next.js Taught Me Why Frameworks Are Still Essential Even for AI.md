# Item 10: Removing Next.js Taught Me Why Frameworks Are Still Essential Even for AI

Source: [https://zenn.dev/smartvain/articles/coding-agent-kills-framework-nextjs-reverse-truth?locale=en](https://zenn.dev/smartvain/articles/coding-agent-kills-framework-nextjs-reverse-truth?locale=en)

Summary:
The author describes an experiment removing Next.js in favor of plain HTML/JS, guided by an AI agent, and concludes that frameworks remain essential in the AI era. While AI can generate boilerplate and implement best practices, the lack of enforced conventions leads to inconsistency and "perceptual gaps" between human and agent. Frameworks provide explicit rules that align both human and AI contributors, making collaboration and long-term maintenance feasible.

Key takeaways:
- Framework conventions act as a shared language for both humans and AI agents.
- Removing frameworks leads to inconsistent code organization, even with AI assistance.
- The primary value of frameworks is shifting from abstraction to convention enforcement in hybrid (human + AI) teams.
- Explicit, enforced conventions are crucial for scalable, maintainable projects.
